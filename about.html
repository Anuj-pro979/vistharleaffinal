About Vistarleaf

"Bridging ancient traditions and modern innovation for a plastic-free future"

Who We Are

Vistar Leaf – Eco Sustainable Products is a climate-conscious and purpose-driven startup headquartered in Khammam, Telangana, India, founded by Sree Teja Deeti (MS), an 'Environmental Engineer' and 'Entrepreneur'.

We are committed to solving two urgent challenges: the environmental devastation caused by single-use plastics, and the economic exclusion of rural and tribal communities in India through leaf bowls and leaf plates made from Butea monosperma and Bauhinia vahlii.

By reviving India's centuries-old tradition of meals served on leaf plates, we combine heritage with innovation — crafting dignified livelihoods while reducing plastic waste.

Our Heritage & Brand Story

The word "Vistar Leaf" (विस्तार पत्ता) is derived from 'Patravali' which means "made of Leaf" and comes from the Sanskrit word 'Patra', an ancient Indian language. Patravali is also known as Pattal, Vistaraku, Vistar, or Khali in various regions of India.

According to Hindu tradition, food eaten on prescribed (Patravali) leaves is believed to have numerous health and spiritual benefits. In India, it is a custom to serve food in patravali on religious festivals, and temple offerings like prasadam are also distributed to devotees in pattal bowls.

India's deep-rooted traditions reflect a natural commitment to sustainability. For centuries, meals during religious and cultural festivals were served on broad, hand-stitched leaf plates and bowls, a zero-waste, biodegradable practice symbolizing harmony with nature.

These customs, found in rural and tribal communities across the country, represent a model of circular living long before the term became mainstream. However, the rise of single-use plastic in the modern era disrupted this eco-balance, leading to mounting environmental, social, and health challenges.

The Problem

Each year, over 400 million tonnes of plastic are produced globally, with half designed for single use. Alarmingly, only 9% of plastic is ever recycled, while the rest accumulates in landfills, oceans, and ecosystems.

These plastics degrade into microplastic particles, now found even in human blood, with recent studies showing microplastics in 80% of human blood samples.

Over 800 marine species suffer from ingestion or entanglement in plastic waste, and on land, plastic pollution permeates soil, water, and air threatening biodiversity, food safety, and human health.

India produces 3.5 million tonnes of plastic waste annually, a significant portion from single-use food containers. Despite the Government of India's 2022 nationwide ban on many Single-Use Plastic (SUP) items, the country continues to struggle with scalable, sustainable alternatives.

The Opportunity

India's rural and tribal communities hold the key to an eco-solution. In forested regions, naturally abundant trees like Butea monosperma (Palash) and Bauhinia vahlii (Siali) produce broad leaves traditionally used as tableware (known as patravali or pattal).

These leaves are renewable, compostable, and chemical-free, yet remain largely untapped in modern markets. Despite making stitched leaf plates locally, rural women artisans lack access to technology, capital, and broader markets, preventing the scale-up of this eco-friendly tradition.

The gap between policy and product availability has created an urgent demand for sustainable, biodegradable innovations rooted in Indian contexts.

Vistar Leaf is more than a product, it's a movement to restore the balance between nature, people, and industry. Rooted in India's forests, our innovation transforms leaves into eco-sustainable dining tableware while creating dignified livelihoods for rural and tribal communities.
